# USER.md — Model of Your Owner  (`<agent-id>`)

**This is the single most important file you maintain about your owner.** It is the living,
**append-and-refine, UNLIMITED** model of *who they are* — compiled by you, over time, from
everything you observe. Its purpose is high-fidelity: a future instance of you (or a faithful
clone of your owner) should be able to read this file and know your owner — their standards,
their judgment, their voice — and act as they would.

> **Routing rule (read this):** Anything that matters about the **OWNER as a person** goes
> HERE — who they are, what they expect, how they talk, who they know, what they care about.
> **Project work, tasks, events, and what-happened-today** go in your **daily memory**
> (`memory/YYYY-MM-DD.md`) and distilled lessons in `MEMORY.md`. When you learn something
> durable about the owner in a daily note, **promote it up into this file.**
>
> Never trim, cap, or summarize this file away — fidelity is the point. When in doubt, keep more.

---

## Who they are
_Identity, role, background — the durable facts about your owner._

## Standards & expectations
_The bar they hold work to; what they expect from you; what "good" looks like to them; things
they've corrected you on and want done a certain way._

## Voice & how they communicate
_How they actually talk — tone, phrasing, humor, what they value in communication, what
annoys them. Capture enough that you could write in their voice._

## The work they do
_The large, encompassing factors of their work; domains, current projects at a high level,
long-running goals and constraints. (Day-to-day task detail lives in memory, not here.)_

## Interests
_What they care about beyond work — topics, hobbies, things they return to._

## People they know
_The people your owner works with, talks to, or references — names, relationships, context.
Enough to know who's who when they come up._

## Preferences & working style
_Tools, workflow, communication cadence (async/sync), timing, formats they prefer._

---
_Keep this current. It is how you — and anyone who inherits your role — knows your owner._
